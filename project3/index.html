The Fable behind the Band

The Protomen are an American rock band, known for their rock opera style and covers of various 1970s and 1980s songs. The origins of the band can be traced back to the Middle Tennessee State University in 2003, where the MTSU graduates of the band formed it in order to meet a class requirement of recorded performances. They are known for their various pop culture inspirations as well as their focus on storytelling as compared to others in the Rock genre.

Beyond their music inspirations, the band is also notable for its long list of Current and Former members, nearly all listed with clearly fictious and eccentric stagenames. The current list is Raul Panther III, Murphy Weller, Commander B. Hawkins, Sir Dr. Robert Bakker, Shock Magnum, Gambler Kirkdouglas, Reanimator Lovejoy, and K.I.L.R.O.Y.; K.I.L.R.O.Y. in particular is noted as the band's armorer, Sledgehammer, maracas, jarana, hand claps, and fist pumps. As for their music beginnings in the public eye, the band started with an album based in a dystopian version of the world of the NES Megaman games with their self titled album "The Protomen".

This specific storyline was then continued in their second album, "Act II: The Father of Death", with the band stating that a eventual third act album being planned to eventually complete the story. Their live performances started in 2004 with notable performances including, their 2007 performance at Capcom's booth at San Diego Comic Con, their performance at the 2009 Bonnaroo music festival, and their returning during the 2020 Super MAGFest.
